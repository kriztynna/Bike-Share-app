why busybici exists

In the summer of 2013, with great enthusiasm I began to use New York City's then-new bike share program for my commute to work. My favorite station was almost always empty, and I found myself fiddling with the mobile app provided to check how many bikes were there before walking over. Most mornings it was depleted, but occassionally I found it freshly refilled, seemingly arbitrarily.

Since I had been learning how to program, I wondered if I could make a little web app that would show me how the volume of available bikes at this station changed over the course of the day or week. But why stop there? Track all the stations, and better yet, send it to my phone on weekday mornings as I'm about to step out.

sharing busybici

busybici doesn't support bike share programs from other cities, but it could! That's why I've shared the project on GitHub. If busybici or any of its components find their way to another city or country, just send me a postcard, ok?
